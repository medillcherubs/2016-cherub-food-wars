Cherubs have multiple options when it comes to food off-campus. Some nights they will have to make a tough choice between two similar establishments. Food Wars is the Evanston guide for cherubs who know what they want to eat, but just can’t decide where. Aspects of each establishment will be dissected and the winner of the most categories takes the round.

Edzo’s vs. Epic Burger

Fries and Shakes

Edzo’s shakes are bigger, but Epic Burger’s shakes are not as clumpy. In fries, however, Edzo’s is the clear winner, with 10 different kinds.

Edge: Edzo’s

Taste

Edzo’s and Epic Burger both offer a wide variety of toppings, and their vegetables are always fresh. The difference is in the burger — while Edzo’s is no slouch, Epic Burger is just slightly juicier.

Slight Edge: Epic Burger

Price

Edzo’s prices are lower than Epic’s for burgers, fries and milkshakes.

Edge: Edzo’s

Verdict: 2-1 Edzo’s

“I feel that Epic Burger is much more of a generic chain, whereas Edzo’s is a one-off. The community there is really unbeatable. It’s about the atmosphere. It’s about the experience.” - Paige Fishman

“I definitely prefer Epic Burger because it’s so farm-fresh and organic. Everything tastes so yummy there. There’s great customer service. The food is delicious.” - Ellie Lieberman

Cold Stone Creamery vs. Andy’s Frozen Custard

Price

They are approximately the same. Andy’s has a wider range of prices, but the really good custard at Andy’s costs more than most of the servings at Cold Stone.

Slight Edge: Cold Stone

Taste

Andy’s is the type of sweet that gets a little sickening after a while, especially since a “small” at Andy’s is the size of a Solo cup. Cold Stone, meanwhile, has the type of taste that never gets old, with more complete toppings. The knock against Cold Stone is its thick consistency, but that’s not enough to overcome its better overall taste.

Edge: Cold Stone

Variety

Both have shakes and malts. Andy’s has Concrete Mixers as opposed to Cold Stone’s smoothies. Andy’s has more kinds of toppings, but Cold Stone has more flavors.

Edge: Cold Stone

Verdict: 3-0 Cold Stone

“I love Andy’s because not only is it very good, it’s very unique. My favorite thing would be the concretes because they’re a smooth blend of everything you love in one bite." - Rachel Friedman

“The flavor choices are better and more varied [at Cold Stone]. And the style of mixing toppings into the ice cream, like brownies and chocolate chips, make it unique and delicious.” - Alexa Silverman

Freshii vs. Lyfe Kitchen

Price

The most expensive bowl at Freshii is $8.99, whereas the least expensive bowl at Lyfe Kitchen is $12. The majority of items on the Freshii menu are under $10, whereas most of Lyfe’s main dishes are more expensive.

Edge: Freshii

Menu

Freshii offers more variety on the menu and Lyfe really loads up ingredients in each dish, so some ingredients can seem unnecessary. Also, Freshii’s smoothie selection is more varied and tasty.

Edge: Freshii

Health Level

If a customer is trying to be healthy, Freshii is a perfect fit. Every burrito has a whole wheat tortilla and brown rice, and every single food item on the menu has a vegetable in it, except for the frozen yogurt. At Lyfe, if a customer wants to stray, it’s possible.

Slight Edge: Freshii

Verdict: 3-0 Freshii

“[Lyfe Kitchen] is really good. I had a chicken avocado sandwich and it was delicious. [It's] some of the best food I've had in Evanston.” - Jane Gormley

"Freshii is a really great place to eat. You can go in and get really healthy food, but also leave without feeling guilty or eating something that feels heavy or sugary. It's great because it's so fresh." - Sophie Rodney
